About Me

Currently, I am a student at North Carolina State University where I am majoring in Electrical Engineering with a concentration in Renewable Electrical Energy Systems & Computer Engineering, and minoring in Sustainable Energy. I aspire to one day work with the Smart Grid dilemma with the hopes of fostering the productivity of renewable energy and promoting sustainability.


My interest in renewable energy was bred from a multitude of experiences early on in my life, the most profound being Scouting. Beginning in the First Grade I was immediately surrounded by a community who appreciated the outdoors. This exposure fostered my affinity for the natural world. As a scout, I grew to love the ever-so-often overlooked gift that is the wilderness that surrounds human environments. Backpacking the Rocky Mountains, sailing through the Florida Keys, and kayaking the North Carolina Outer Banks were all contributing experiences that led me to further appreciate the natural wonders present in our world today.


Around the same time I began my Scouting journey, I also uncovered my love for computers. My interest developed from the enthusiasm of the media specialist at OLG, Jacky Hanner. In “Computer Class” we designed clay-mations, advanced our typing skills, and even developed our own html webpages. I drew a particular interest in code and web design. My love for coding flourished and my exploration of the concepts behind the Internet and computers has yet to end.


A group project in an Environmental Science high school course exposed me to renewable energy and how this technology has the potential to sustainably support all of our energy needs. This idea fascinated me, and led me to begin researching the topic outside of school. The intricacies of how electricity is generated and transmitted drove me to research the seemingly endless complexities of the entire energy field. I sprouted a desire to pursue real world change through altering the way humans use and receive energy.


Later in high school and into the beginning of my postsecondary education I began to consider what major I wanted to pursue. While most undergraduate students first pick a major that matches their interests and later fall under a career-applicable field within that major, I found myself in the opposite position. I’d already discovered the real world issue I wanted to tackle, my setback was unearthing the major that would best maximize my potential, given my interests, at making a noticeable and a real impact.


I found that I could apply my three pronounced interests – nature, computers, and energy – towards one common goal – the mitigation of climate change, through Electrical Engineering. Specifically, my pursuit of the Renewable Electric Energy Systems concentration offered at North Carolina State University will provide me the tools necessary to offer real world solutions to instantaneously supplying renewably generated electricity all across the United States through Smart Grid technologies.


I aspire to be a forefront leader in the development and implementation of emerging renewable technologies. I believe that through the development of renewable energy sources it will be possible to, one day, cut ties with petroleum, coal and other toxic finite energy sources. Nothing makes me more passionate than the opportunity to be a part of this change.
