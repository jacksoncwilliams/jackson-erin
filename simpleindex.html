About Me: 1

My interest in renewable energy was bred from a multitude of experiences early on in my life, the most profound being Scouting. Beginning in the First Grade I was immediately surrounded by a community who appreciated the outdoors. This exposure fostered my affinity for the natural world. As a scout, I grew to love the ever so often-overlooked gift that is the wilderness that surrounds human environments. Backpacking the Rocky Mountains, sailing through the Florida Keys, and kayaking the North Carolina Outer Banks were all contributing experiences that led me to further appreciate the natural wonders out there in our world today. Now as an Eagle Scout, I’m aware of the constant human destruction to our planet. I want to do something about it.

Around the same time I began my Scouting journey, I also uncovered my love for computers. By age 9 I had created my first html website and by age 15 I had professionally developed websites for over seven reputable organizations. My love for coding flourished and my exploration of the concepts behind the Internet and computers has yet to end. As I grew as a student I began to further uncover factual evidence of the degradation of the Earth’s health. Specifically, I developed an interest in energy and how humans are wastefully burning an absurd amount of fossil fuels. I sprouted a desire to pursue real world change through altering the way humans use and receive energy. An Environmental Science high school course exposed me to renewable energy and how this technology has the capability of sustainably supporting all of our energy needs. This idea fascinated me, and continues to drive my interest in this field.

Later in high school and into the beginning of my postsecondary education I began to consider what career I wanted to pursue. While most undergraduate students first pick a major that matches their interests and later on fall under a career-applicable field within that major, I found myself in the opposite position. I’d already discovered the real world issue I wanted to tackle, my setback was unearthing the major that would best maximize my potential at making a noticeable and profound impact to the issue I am passionately devoted.

I found that I could apply my three pronounced interests – nature, computers, and energy – towards one common goal – the mitigation of climate change, through Electrical Engineering. Specifically, my pursuit of the Renewable Electric Energy Systems concentration offered at North Carolina State University will provide me the tools necessary to offer real world solutions to instantaneously supplying renewably generated electricity all across the United States through Smart Grid technologies.

I aspire to be a forefront leader on the development and implementation of emerging renewable technologies such as photovoltaic solar panels, wind energy turbines, hydrothermal power, and geothermal energy. I believe that through the development of renewable energy sources it will be possible to cut ties with petroleum and other harmful finite energy sources and thus end the energy crisis thereby playing a lead role in the reversal of climate change. Nothing makes me more passionate than the opportunity to be apart of this change.
